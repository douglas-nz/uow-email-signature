Create your University of Waikato email signature

This is a template email signature. If this doesn't cover all of the information you have in your current signature, you can add extra information manually when you copy the signature over into your email settings.

To create your University email signature, choose your email client and follow the steps.

My email client of choice is

Gmail Users

  1. Fill out the form below.
  2. Once all the required fields have been completed click the Copy Signature button.
  3. Go to Gmail and select Settings > See All Settings. Scroll down to Signature.
  4. Delete your current signature and paste the new one into the text box.
  5. In Signature defaults make sure you select your Waikato email address and assign your newly created signature in the FOR NEW EMAILS USE section.
  6. Scroll to the bottom of Settings and click Save Changes.
  7. Send a quick test email to make sure it's working.

Outlook Users

  1. Fill out the form below.
  2. Once all the required fields have been completed click the Copy Signature button.
  3. Open a New Email message in Outlook.
  4. Select Insert, click the Signature drop down menu.
  5. Select Signatures and select a signature to edit (or create a new one).
  6. Paste your new signature into the text box.
  7. Click OK.
  8. Click Save then OK to finish.
  9. Send a quick test email to make sure it’s working.

Apple Mail Users

  1. Fill out the form below.
  2. Once all the required fields have been completed click the Copy Signature button.
  3. Go to Apple Mail and from the top menu select Preferences.
  4. *Important* Uncheck the "Always match my default message font" checkbox.
  5. If you have an existing signature, click on the signature name in the centre column and paste in your new signature.
  6. If you don't have an existing signature click the + button, give your signature a name, click into the third column and paste in your new signature.
  7. At the bottom of the preferences screen in the "Choose Signature" dropdown, select your new signature.
